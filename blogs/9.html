GMAT v. GMAT Focus Edition: Which One Should You Opt For?

The world of business education is constantly evolving, and with it, the standardized tests used for admissions. The Graduate Management Admission Test (GMAT) is a critical exam for individuals seeking admission to graduate business programs, such as MBA courses. Over time, the GMAT has seen revisions to better suit the needs of both test takers and the schools they apply to.

The latest development is the introduction of the GMAT Focus Edition â€“ a new version of the GMAT. If you're contemplating taking the GMAT, you may find yourself faced with a dilemma: Should you opt for the familiar current GMAT or venture into the uncharted territory of the GMAT Focus Edition? Let's delve into the differences between these two exams to help you make an informed decision.

Current GMAT:

  • Adaptive Nature: The hallmark of the current GMAT is its adaptive nature. As you progress through each section, the test adapts to your performance. Correct answers lead to progressively more challenging questions, while incorrect responses lead to easier ones.

  • Content Diversity: The current GMAT is known for its broad content coverage. From critical reasoning and data interpretation to problem-solving and grammar, it assesses a wide range of skills essential for success in business school.

  • Structure and Sections: The current GMAT comprises four main sections: Analytical Writing Assessment (AWA), Integrated Reasoning (IR), Quantitative Reasoning (QR), and Verbal Reasoning (VR).

  • Duration & Sections - The duration of the exam is 3 hours and 7 minutes spanning across 4 sections namely Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, Integrated Reasoning and Analytical Writing Assessment.

  • Evaluation and Score Range - The test has three different scores in the range of 200-800.

    • GMAT Focus Edition:

  • Concise Duration: Compared to the current GMAT, the GMAT Focus Edition is generally shorter in duration. This is because it hones in on assessing your skills in specific areas, resulting in a more time-efficient testing experience.

  • Personalized Assessment: The GMAT Focus Edition takes personalization to the next level. It adapts to your individual strengths and weaknesses, selecting questions that target areas where improvement is needed.

  • Focused Question Types: Unlike the current GMAT, which covers a wide variety of question types, the GMAT Focus Edition narrows its focus to specific question types. This concentrated approach is particularly beneficial if you want to target particular areas of strength or weakness.

  • Personalized Preparation Recommendations: The GMAT Focus Edition doesn't just end with the exam. It provides tailored recommendations based on your performance, guiding your preparation efforts and helping you allocate time to areas that need attention.

  • Instant Score Reporting: One of the standout features of the GMAT Focus Edition is its provision of an immediate unofficial score at the conclusion of the test. This real-time feedback can be invaluable for making decisions related to your application strategy.

  • Duration & Sections - The duration of the exam is 2 hours and 15 minutes spanning across 3 sections namely Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Data Insights.

  • Evaluation and Score Range - The test has a single unified score in the range of 205-805.

    Commonly Asked Questions:

  • How does the GMAT Focus Edition adapt to my skills?

    • The GMAT Focus Edition adapts through its question selection. If you answer a question correctly, the subsequent questions might be more challenging in the same skill area. If you answer incorrectly, the exam might offer easier questions in that area, allowing it to determine your proficiency more precisely.

  • Is the GMAT Focus Edition suitable for all test takers?

    • The GMAT Focus Edition might be more suitable for individuals who want a targeted assessment, have limited time for testing, or wish to focus on specific skills. However, those seeking a comprehensive evaluation across all areas might prefer the current GMAT.

  • Are the question types on the GMAT Focus Edition different?

    • Yes, the GMAT Focus Edition emphasizes specific question types based on your skills. This targeted approach sets it apart from the current GMAT, which covers a broader range of question types.

  • How should I prepare for the GMAT Focus Edition?

    • Preparing for the GMAT Focus Edition involves understanding your strengths and weaknesses, focusing on the recommended areas, and practicing the question types emphasized in the exam. The personalized recommendations provided after the exam can guide your preparation strategy.

  • Can I choose which GMAT version to take?

    • Yes, you can choose between the current GMAT and the GMAT Focus Edition based on your preferences and needs. Consider your study plan, areas of focus, and the requirements of the business schools you're applying to when making your decision.

  • Do schools accept scores from the GMAT Focus Edition?

    • Most business schools that accept GMAT scores will likely accept scores from the GMAT Focus Edition. For 2024 intake, most B-schools are sticking with the current GMAT; hence, if you're planning to apply for 2024 intake, you should opt for the current edition.

  • Can I switch between the GMAT versions if I change my mind?

    • The availability of both GMAT versions might depend on the testing centers and locations. Before registering, check if the testing center offers both versions and whether you can switch between them if needed.

  • How often is the GMAT Focus Edition offered?

    • The frequency of GMAT Focus Edition test administrations might vary by location and demand. Check with the official GMAT website or your testing center for information about the availability of the GMAT Focus Edition in your area.

    Which GMAT version should I take?

    When deciding whether to take the current GMAT or the GMAT Focus Edition, consider the following factors:

  • Your Target Intake: If you're planning to apply for 2024 intake, you should opt for the current GMAT. If you're planning to apply for 2025 intake (& onwards), you can opt between any of the two editions- the following points can help you choose between the two!

  • Time Constraints: If time is of the essence and you're seeking a quicker testing experience, the GMAT Focus Edition's shorter duration and immediate score feedback could be advantageous.

  • Comprehensive vs. Targeted Evaluation: If you seek a comprehensive evaluation of your skills across a wide spectrum, the current GMAT is the route to take. On the other hand, if you're looking to fine-tune specific abilities, the GMAT Focus Edition offers a more targeted approach.

  • Study Plan Alignment: Your study plan and available preparation time can play a pivotal role in your decision. The personalized recommendations offered by the GMAT Focus Edition can assist in structuring your study strategy effectively.

    • In conclusion, the choice between the current GMAT and the GMAT Focus Edition depends on your individual preferences, strengths, weaknesses, and time constraints. The two exams serve different purposes and cater to diverse test-taking styles. Both are valuable tools for showcasing your aptitude for graduate business studies. It's important to assess your goals, study approach, and the expectations of the business schools you're targeting. Ultimately, the decision hinges on which exam format aligns best with your overall strategy for success.
